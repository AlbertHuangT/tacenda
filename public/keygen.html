Identity Key Generator

generate a persistent RSA-2048 key pair for long-term identity

Before you generate

An identity key pair is optional. It lets you establish recurring contact with someone without exchanging fresh public keys every session.

Your long-term public key is your identity. Share it with contacts once, offline. Your long-term private key must be kept safe — anyone who obtains it can impersonate you and intercept handshakes addressed to you.

⚠ Key = Identity: your public key is your only identifier in this system. There are no usernames, no emails, no account recovery. Contacts address messages to your public key — it is your address.

If you generate a new key pair for any reason, you effectively create a new identity. All existing contact relationships become one-way: you can still send messages to anyone whose public key you have, but they will be unable to reach you — their messages will be addressed to your old key, which no longer exists.

This remains broken until you proactively contact each person and share your new public key. Treat key generation as irreversible — back up your private key carefully.

Note: this page generates keys inside your browser using the Web Crypto API. JavaScript is delivered by the server — a compromised server could theoretically intercept the key before it is downloaded. For higher assurance, use the CLI instead: ./tacenda-cli --keygen

Private key  —  download and store securely, never share

Save identity.pem somewhere only you can access. Do not upload it anywhere. If you lose it, generate a new pair and inform your contacts.

After generating

Share your public key with each contact once — in person, over Signal, or any channel you trust. They will use it to address encrypted handshakes to you.

To use your identity key, run the CLI: 

./tacenda-cli --key identity.pem --server wss://ppb1s0n.us.kg/ws
